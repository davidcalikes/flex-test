Band Biography

How it started

In 2016 three musicians met one stormy thursday night in the world famous Gartlans Thatched pub in Kingscourt Co.Cavan

It was during one of the venue's now legendary Thursday night Trad sessions, Local troubadour Liam Lawman accompanied by nearby Maheraclone banjo player Andrew Kelly first caught the attention of Glaswegian songsmith David Calikes.

After many rounds, songs and stories were shared the inception of the band became almost inevitable.

Many a garage jam session later and it became even clearer that conceptually and musically these three talented performers were about to embark on an authentic and meaningful creative journey.

How it's Going

Cumasc Gael (Celtic fusion in Gaelic) is a reference not only to the Scottish and Irish provenence of its members, but also to the unique fusion of Traditional Irish and Scots folk music with raucous Country-Blues, Americana and Bluegrass.

Since those early days in Gartlans and many packed out venues and festivals in the local North-East, area the group have gone from strength to strength, entertaining a growing, passionate fanbase wherever they travel.

And travel they have. Just these past few month the band has performed across the eastern seaboard including venues in Washington D.C. Charlotte N.C. and Miami, Florida. Back on home soil, you can find a list of upcoming dates on our Homepage. (Further European dates TBA)
